Upon reading the article, "Best practices for form design" by Salim Ansari,
I've come to truly appreciate and understand the vision behind the
Inclusive Product Design Movement, one that i've been following
since the start of my fascination with Product Management.

Inclusive Product Design focuses on shifting the paradigm of the design
process to include diversity & inclusivity from the very beginning of the
product design life cycle, and designing for all types of users--not just one.

Thus, the section of the article titled "Accessibility" stood out to me in
particular. The section highlights key details regarding improving
accessibility for forms, especially in regards to color, text-size, and labels.

For instance, in regards to color, Ansari details how designers should
be mindful of ensuring that the contrast between the foreground
and background meet the minimum contrast ratio as instituted
by the WCAG, as well as not using solely color to indicate changes
in status to account for the color-blind population of users.

The author has insightful perspective in regards to empathizing with
the accessibility troubles of minority groups, an understanding every
designer would hope to develop!
